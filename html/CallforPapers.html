Call For Papers

It’s our pleasure to hold the first workshop on Document Image and Language (DIL). Multi-modality in artificial intelligence has become a promising trend nowadays and NLP technology has gained a rapid development in recent years. In order to accelerate the utilization of text modality in document image understanding, we provide this platform for the frontier works on the combination of these two areas. The workshop aims to link the researchers from both document image understanding and NLP communities to bring more inspiration. We encourage the authors to submit papers on the topics below.

Topics:

  • * OCR with Semantic Model

    1.Post-OCR text correction

    2.Semantic text recognition

    3.Semantic text spotting

  • * NLP Technologies on Document

    1.Document entity recognition

    2.Document entity relationship

    3.Document visual question answering (DocVQA)

    4.Text classification

    5.Message and table understanding

    6.Document sentiment analysis

    7.Text similarity measures

    8.Fact or relationship extraction

  • * Multi-Modality Document Image Analysis and Recognition

    1.Pretrained model for document analysis

    2.Language model for document information extraction

    3.Document layout understanding

    4.Graphic and formula recognition in document

    5.Medical document image recognition

    6.Document classification

    7.Document summarization and translation

Submission:

The first DIL is calling for original and unpublished papers on topics of interest as above. Papers have to be submitted via the workshop's Easychair submission page. We will follow a single blind review process and each paper will be reviewed by at least two reviewers. Since this year, the ICDAR main conference planned to publish the proceedings with Springer LNCS, papers should be formatted according to LNCS instructions for authors.

We welcome the following types of contributions:

  • ■ Full research papers (12-15 pages): finished or consolidated R&D works, to be included in one of the Workshop topics
  • ■ Short papers (6-8 pages): ongoing works with relevant preliminary results, opened to discussion.
